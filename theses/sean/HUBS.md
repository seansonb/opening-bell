# HubSpot (HUBS) Investment Thesis

## Core Bull Case
The market is mispricing HUBS by overweighting the threat of AI-enabled 
DIY software. While vibe-coding tools make it technically possible for 
companies to build their own CRM, the operational reality of hosting, 
maintaining, and staffing custom tooling makes this impractical for the 
vast majority of businesses. Established SaaS vendors like HubSpot will 
absorb AI capabilities and deliver more value per dollar, not less. The 
$1B buyback at current prices signals management agreement that the stock 
is materially undervalued.

## This Thesis Applies Broadly To
SaaS companies serving SMB and mid-market with high switching costs. 
See: macro/saas_disruption_thesis.md

## Key Assumptions
1. SMB and mid-market companies will not meaningfully self-build CRM/marketing 
   tooling even as AI lowers the build cost
2. HubSpot will successfully integrate AI features that increase platform 
   stickiness and ARPU rather than being disrupted by them
3. Churn rates remain stable or improve as switching costs increase with 
   deeper AI feature adoption
4. The "build vs buy" calculus continues to favor buy for companies 
   under ~$500M revenue

## Thesis Invalidators
These specific developments would force a re-evaluation:
- Sustained NRR decline below 100% for 2+ consecutive quarters
- Churn acceleration explicitly attributed to customers building 
  in-house replacements (watch earnings call language carefully)
- A credible AI-native CRM competitor gaining meaningful enterprise share
- HubSpot failing to ship competitive AI features within 2 product cycles
- SMB formation rates declining materially (macro invalidator)

## Key Metrics to Monitor
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) — single most important number
- Seat growth vs ARPU growth (growing by users or by value?)
- Churn rate and stated churn reasons from earnings calls
- AI feature adoption and Breeze agent usage metrics
- Competitive win/loss mentions on earnings calls
- $1B buyback execution pace — are they actually buying?
